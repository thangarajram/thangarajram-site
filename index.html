About Me

I am a PhD student at the New Jersey Institute of Technology, advised by Dr. Alisha Pradhan. I conduct Human-Computer Interaction research, with a focus on aging. More specifically, I am interested in what happens to our personal data after we die — post-mortem data management. My work predominantly involves conducting interviews and design workshops with adults aged 65 and above. Additionally, I am also interested in digital identity expression among South Asian users through non-verbal communication media such as emoji skin tone modifiers and GIFs.

Prior to moving to the U.S. for my PhD, I completed my Master’s in User Experience Design at Birmingham City University (UK). I also worked as a management consultant (healthcare and oil & gas supply chain optimization) at Ernst and Young LLP in India for over four years. Before joining EY, I completed my Bachelor’s in Naval Architecture and Ocean Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras (India).

In my free time, I like to volunteer/organize workshops for helping seniors and returning citizens with technology, write poetry (recently published my poetry collection), perform poetry at open mics, and play a few musical instruments (ukulele, drums, and keyboard). Recently, I started exploring acrylic painting, mainly still life subjects.
